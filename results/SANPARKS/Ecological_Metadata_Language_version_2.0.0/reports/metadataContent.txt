SANPARKS|00343-metadata.xml|/ns0:eml/dataset/methods/methodStep/description/section/para|The method for determining the number of animals and species utilizing the EBP is a relatively simple one and will consist of driving along the plots on a daily basis at differing but rotational times and recording the number of animals, the activity the animal is undertaking,(either resting grazing or unknown.ie passing through, and finally recording the species of animals on each plot.(see fig 1). The distance to each animal or group of animals will also be determined by means of a laser rangefinder, to enable the observers to calculate animal density per unit area.Some of the plots differ in that they lay on both sides of the road. In this scenario the sides that are furthest away from the road will be monitored. Seeing that an experiment in a specific vegetation type consists of four replications of 12 treatments, e.g. four blocks of plots lying some distance apart, the route travelled for making observations will have to go along all four blocks. To avoid visiting the same plots or blocks at the same time every day the route taken each day will be varied, thus ensuring some degree of randomisation as far as time of observation at a specific plot can be obtained. Figure 2 illustrates a route covering all the plots and replicates of specific vegetation type in a randomised fashion.

SANPARKS|00421-metadata.xml|/ns0:eml/dataset/abstract/para|The ‘Nwashitsumbe vlei in the Kruger National Park is separated from a mopane matrix by the vlei/upland boundary. The boundary has been delineated in terms of grass species and soil characteristics, but not woody vegetation, which is an integral component of savanna ecosystems. A large exclosure provided the opportunity to study the effects of large mammalian browser exclusion on boundary dynamics and woody vegetation characteristics. The vlei/upland boundary differs from the surrounding patch types in species composition, slope, electrical conductivity and pH characteristics. Each characteristic has a slightly different spatial distribution pattern across the boundary, probably resulting from differential responses to different or the same vectors and disturbance agents. These characters should be viewed holistically as they overlap to form a complex three-dimensional boundary. Woody species distribution is more patchy than that of the grasses. Life-history strategy responses are suggested as possible causes for differences in distribution pattern. Browsing disturbance has altered the structure, composition and possibly the function of the woody vegetation boundary. This study suggests that the vlei/upland boundary is more sensitive to large mammalian herbivore impact than the surrounding patch types. Such changes and differences are of concern to managers who aim to maintain biodiversity. Current monitoring, in the Kruger National park, of woody vegetation responses to herbivore impact occurs at too coarse a scale to detect change in either the boundary or the species guild-specific responses identified in this study. It is recommended that they be used as indicators of herbivore mediated change in the landscape.

SANPARKS|00421-metadata.xml|/ns0:eml/dataset/methods/methodStep/description/section/para|Boundaries are locations where the rates or magnitudes of ecological transfers change abruptly in relation to those within the surrounding patch types (Harrod 1988). Boundaries can therefore be identified though discontinuities or regions of high variation in environmental or vegetation characteristics. Locations of high variance can be determined using a moving split-window technique (Ludwig &mp;amp; Cornelis 1987).
SANPARKS|00421-metadata.xml|/ns0:eml/dataset/methods/methodStep/description/section/para|Window size refers to the number of samples or quadrats for which a change in variance is evaluated. The mean for each half-window is subtracted from the mean of the other half-window to determine variance between the two-half windows in terms of Euclidean distance. The window is then moved one quadrat or sample point along a transect and the process repeated until the entire transect has been sampled. Various half-window sizes were experimented with. A size of three quadrats or sample points (i.e. 7.5m) was selected for the scale of the study.
